📚 ReHPAI tool

Reviewing Healthcare Papers with AI

Step 1: The editorial invite

Ask yourself the following questions:

  1. Do I have the expertise to review this manuscript?
  2. Do I have any conflicts of interest?
  3. Do I have time to complete the review before the deadline? (ask for an extension if this is not the case)
Try to respond to the invite as soon as possible to not keep the editor waiting. Also add the reason why you rejected if applicable.

🔍 AI-specific Considerations

Step 2: Paper first read

Read the paper for the first time. Note anything that comes to mind while reading the manuscript, which you can later categorise further:

  1. Which elements do you lack background on? Search for this information in step 3.
  2. Thoughts/concerns to include in your review. You can later decide whether they are a reason to reject the manuscript, major, minor, suggestions or not worth mentioning after all. They can also be internal notes for the editor.
🔍 AI-specific Considerations

Step 3: Literature search (optional)

Additional literature review is warranted in two cases:

  1. It is unclear to you whether the manuscript represent original research not published before.
  2. You lack background on certain paper elements as identified in step 2.

🔍 AI-specific Considerations

Step 4: Reread the paper

Rereading the manuscript offers a new perspective. Therefore, Sedaghat et al. 2024 suggest to wait at least a day with rereading to have a fresh pair of eyes. The same group of authors actually suggest you to reread twice, once from a scientific point of view, once for the writing. The frequency is up to you, but rereading allows you to filter your initial notes from step 2 and add new considerations.

Step 5: Classify your notes

Start from the notes you took in step 2 and refined in step 4. Categorise the notes:

  • Remark that justifies rejection
  • Major remark
  • Minor remark
  • Suggestion
  • Not worth mentioning after all

🔍 AI-specific Considerations

Step 6: Write your review report

A good visual summary for writing your review report can be found in figure 2 of Sedaghat et al. 2024. Concretely, it consists of a summary paragraph, followed by a section on major remarks and one on minor remarks. If certain notes from step 5 arised that are for the eyes of the editor only, include this in your letter to the editor, which is not shared with the reviewers.

🔍 AI-specific Considerations

Expertise. Receiving a request to review a paper involving AI while you have little expertise in AI doesn't mean an automatic reject. Ask yourself whether this paper has a heavy emphasis on AI development:

  • Yes: Contact the editor to ask whether another reviewer covers this aspect:
    • Yes: You can focus on domain-specific considerations.
    • No: It is probably recommended to reject to review.
  • No: It is likely that the editor contacted you for domain-specific knowledge. You can safely assume you have the required expertise.
